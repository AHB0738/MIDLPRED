MIDLPred: Leveraging Deep Learning on Protein Sequences to Uncover Male Infertility Factors

BEN DAHMANE Abdelhafedh*,1, BENSAADA Mustapha1, CHEHILI Hamza1, ALIOUANE Salah eddine1, HAMIDECHI Mohamed Abdelhafid2

1. Department of Biology, Laboratory of Microbial Engineering and Applications, University of Constantine 1, Constantine, Algeria.
2. Department of Biology, Abbes Laghrour Khenchela University, Khenchela, Algeria.
*Correspondence: abdelhafedh.ben-dahmane@doc.umc.edu.dz

Associate Editor: XXXXXXX

Motivation

This work presents MIDLPred, a classification model based on deep learning (DL) and natural language processing (NLP), designed to predict male infertility factors from primary protein sequences.

Methods

Using a dataset of 10,678 sequences from the MIK database annotated according to four clinical classes (MOTILITY, SHAPE, PHYSIO, OTHER), we developed a one-dimensional convolutional neural network (1D-CNN) architecture. Optimization was achieved via cross-validation and enhanced through an ensemble learning strategy involving ten independent models.

Results

The model achieved a weighted F1-score of 0.96, with accuracy greater than 98% for certain classes. Confusion matrices and ROC curves demonstrated excellent generalization across all categories. MIDLPred also identified relevant molecular patterns related to infertility phenotypes, consistent with current knowledge in reproductive genetics.

Conclusion

MIDLPred represents a significant advance in the application of artificial intelligence (AI) to molecular andrology. It provides a robust, scalable, and interpretable method for automated classification of male infertility factors from protein sequences, paving the way for more accurate and personalized diagnostics.
